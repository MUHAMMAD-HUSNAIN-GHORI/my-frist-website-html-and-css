About the University of Sindh, Jamshoro


The University of Sindh, Jamshoro, is a prestigious institution of higher education in Pakistan, founded in 1947. It stands as one of the country's oldest universities, renowned for its rich history and commitment to academic excellence.


History and Evolution

Initially established to meet the educational needs of the region, the University of Sindh has evolved into a comprehensive institution offering a wide range of programs. Its foundation is rooted in the University of Sindh Act of 1947, which was revised in 1961 and 1972 to provide greater autonomy and representation for teachers.

Academic Programs

The university offers a diverse array of undergraduate, graduate, and postgraduate programs through its various faculties, which include:

  • Arts and Humanities:English, Fine Arts, Literature, Philosophy, Urdu, and more
  • Natural Sciences: Anthropology, Biochemistry, Computer Science, Geology, Mathematics, Microbiology, Physics, Zoology, etc.
  • Social Sciences: Economics, History, Political Science, Psychology, Sociology, and more.
  • Commerce and Business Administration:Business Administration, Commerce.
  • EducationPrimary Education, Higher Education, Distance Education.
  • Islamic Studies:Comparative Religion, History of Religion, Islamic Studies
  • Law: Comprehensive legal studies.
  • Pharmacy:Pharmacology, Pharmaceutical sciences.

Facilities and Campus Life

The Jamshoro campus is equipped with modern facilities to support academic and extracurricular activities:

  • Central Library: A resource-rich library offering a vast collection of academic materials.
  • Research Laboratories: State-of-the-art labs for cutting-edge research.
  • Sports Complex: Comprehensive facilities for a variety of sports.
  • Residential Halls: Comfortable and secure housing for students.
  • Cultural Museum: A repository showcasing the cultural heritage of Sindh.
  • Health Services: On-campus medical care for students and staff.
  • Other Amenities: Including canteens, a mini-hospital, a data center, and a university colony.
